Pixel Art

Introduction

Pixel art is often used in video games despite modern capabilities to create more well-defined and detailed depictions of text, characters, and landscapes. The appearance of pixelated art looks choppy and gives a very simple image of said examples. There are several instances in which text, characters, and landscapes are used in an artistic way in media, including movies, TV shows, and classic paintings when it comes to choices regarding the color palette, the focus points, and the feeling the compositions invoke.

Text

Different forms of writing can be considered an art as much as images. Commonly known examples are graffiti and calligraphy, but pixel art is another form of font style art that can be overlooked. It gives off a vintage feel, like old arcade games, and game developers take advantage of this nostalgiac feeling in their art.

Characters

Pixelated appearances throughout the player's journey helps them to fill in the blanks with their own imagination where the details are lacking. This gives pixel art a new effect of differing interpretation. Also, if the characters are ever in closer contact, the game may have you see them in a new light in which they are more detailed and show more aspects of their personality/appearance that are notable.

Landscapes

The unrealistic nature of pixel art makes room for more whimsy and less of a need for sensible design, allowing for more abstract compositions. Without as much detail, players are still submerged into this world and its simplistic design, so vast landscapes are more easily regarded as impressive since it's a part of the world the developers built.
